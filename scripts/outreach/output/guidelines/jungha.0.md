## Content Do's
*   **Focus on the "Champagne" Factor:** Treat the Sparkling Makgeolli as a premium alternative to Champagne. Use flutes or clean glassware.
*   **Minimalist Backgrounds:** Use the brand colors (Warm White, Volcanic Black). Stone textures, linen, and soft shadows are encouraged.
*   **Show the Texture:** For the Son Makgeolli, capture the creamy, unfiltered "cloudiness" of the liquid.
*   **Natural Lighting:** Even if shot in a studio, the lighting should mimic the soft, golden hour glow of Jeju Island.
*   **English/Korean Mix:** Maintain her signature bilingual caption style to appeal to her international and domestic fashion-forward followers.

## Content Don'ts
*   **No "Anju" Clutter:** Avoid traditional, heavy Korean pub food (No *pajeon* or *jeyuk-bokkeum*). Keep food styling minimalâ€”think a single pear, a piece of sourdough, or premium cheese.
*   **No "Party" Vibes:** Avoid neon lights, crowded tables, or "cheersing" with large groups. This is about personal indulgence and craft.
*   **No Heavy Filters:** Avoid high-contrast or "beauty" filters that distort the natural ivory color of the Makgeolli.
*   **No Generic Posing:** Avoid the "holding the bottle next to the face" smile. Opt for candid, editorial movements.

## Posting Schedule & Format
*   **Primary Format:** 1x Instagram Feed Carousel (4-5 slides).

    *   Slide 2: Detail shot of the bottle/label.
